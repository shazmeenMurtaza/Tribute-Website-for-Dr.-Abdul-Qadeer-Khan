Biography of Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan (1936 – 2021) was a Pakistani nuclear scientist and metallurgical engineer, widely celebrated as the father of Pakistan’s nuclear program. He was born on April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, India, and migrated to Pakistan after the partition in 1952.

He completed his education in Europe, earning degrees in metallurgy and later a doctorate in engineering. In the 1970s, he returned to Pakistan and played a pivotal role in developing the nation’s nuclear capability, making Pakistan the first Islamic country to have nuclear weapons.

Dr. Khan’s efforts gave Pakistan a strong position on the world stage and ensured its defense capabilities. For his contributions, he was awarded Pakistan’s highest civilian honors, including Nishan-e-Imtiaz and Hilal-e-Imtiaz.

He passed away on October 10, 2021, but remains a national hero and symbol of resilience and scientific achievement for Pakistan.
